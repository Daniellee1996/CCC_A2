Total count of twitter about Covid-19 by cities

Twitter about Covid-19 The first visualized pie chart indicated the percentage of tweets which related to the covid-19 topic by cities. As the pie chart illustrates, the Covid-19 issue is one of the most popular topics in Melbourne, followed by Sydney, Brisbane, Gold Coast, Perth and Adelaide. This trend can reflect the total amount of population in this city that the number of tweets about Covid-19 is positively correlated with its population. However, the correlation is not strong enough since the population of Melbourne is ranked 2nd among Australian cities while the percentage Melbourne taken is more than Sydney. In this situation, the explanation requires to drill down the significant issues happening in Melbourne and in-turn the result will be altered. It was found that Melbourne experienced a 112 day long lockdown which can trigger community members to focus more in Covid-19 issue in social media (BBC news, 2020). This is the key reason for why the percentage of mentioning Covid-19 in Melbourne outweighs Sydney which has the most population in Australia. This can be considered as evidence that the number of Covid-19 oriented Tweets also has positive correlation with the extent of lockdown and the number of confirmed cases. Another interesting finding is that the proportion of Covid-19 oriented Tweets in Brisbane and Gold Coast are very similar, however the number of populations between Brisbane and Gold Coast is significant which is 2.28 million and 569,997. ABS (2019) stated that the percentage of seniors in Gold Coast was 16.6% which is higher than the national average of 15.9% in 2019. The serious ageing of population structure in Gold Coast is the major motivation for the high number of Covid-19 tweets since seniors aged 65 and above is the most age group which is sensitive to exposure of Covid-19.
